You're a psychopath without it.”

“The landscape Mikhael saw was breathtaking. Hues of grey, blue, and red coalesced with the blackness of space into the most magnificent sight. And Mikhael was the first man to gaze upon this with naked eyes; in fact, the only man.”

—Hyperion

“Then the devil appeared. He asked if I wanted to dance. I said yes. I grabbed his hand and he twirled me around. We danced for so long I lost track of time.”

“If indeed the Revolutionary war was made for ideals—the ideals of liberty, people’s rule and equality—then the Founding Father’s policies after separation suggests that these notions were simply forgotten. The majority of persons in North America—Native Americans, African-Americans, women—did not gain liberty nor representation; in fact, their situation did not improve after the war.”

—A Crisis of Conscience

“The Earth is one huge turtle, as one Mandan creation myth goes, and one time it sank because the white men stuck a knife through its shell. Water flooded its back, but fortunately the tribe had built a tower on the lower bank of the Heart River, and thus was saved.”

—A Flooded Homeland: The Mandan, Hidatsa, and Arikara Tribes

“He held the knife to her throat. It was cold against her skin. With a flick of his wrist, he could end her life; all of her hopes, fears, and dreams would disappear in moments; her baby boy would never see her again; and she would never be able to tell the world what happened. She started to cry. He started to laugh.”

—The Crossdresser
